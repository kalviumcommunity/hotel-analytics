# Data Dictionary

## Dataset Overview

This dataset contains customer transaction records updated daily from the CRM system.

- Last Updated: 2025-05-21
- Maintained By: Data Engineering Team

---

# Columns

## customer_id

- Type: Integer
- Business Meaning: Unique customer identifier from CRM system
- Example: 12456
- Null Handling: Never null (primary key)
- Related KPI: Customer tracking, customer lifetime value
- Updates: Assigned when customer is created

---

## trnx_amt

- Type: Float
- Business Meaning: Revenue generated by a single transaction
- Example: 150.99
- Unit: USD
- Null Handling: Investigate if null values appear
- Related KPI:
  - Monthly revenue
  - Average transaction value
  - Customer lifetime value
- Updates: Generated when a transaction completes

---

## purchase_date

- Type: Datetime
- Business Meaning: Date when transaction occurred
- Example: 2025-01-15
- Null Handling: Never null
- Related KPI:
  - Sales velocity
  - Monthly revenue
- Updates: Recorded automatically

---

## cust_segment

- Type: String
- Business Meaning: Customer market segment
- Valid Values:
  - B2B
  - B2C
  - SMB
- Example: B2B
- Null Handling: If null, classify as UNKNOWN
- Related KPI:
  - Segment revenue
  - Segment profitability
- Updates: Monthly from CRM

---

## flag_churn

- Type: Integer
- Business Meaning: Indicates whether customer churned within 90 days
- Example: 0
- Valid Values:
  - 0 = No
  - 1 = Yes
- Related KPI:
  - Churn rate
  - Retention analysis
- Updates: Quarterly

---

# Column to KPI Mapping

## Monthly Revenue

- Formula: SUM(trnx_amt)
- Related Columns: trnx_amt, purchase_date
- Why It Matters: Tracks company revenue
- Update Frequency: Daily

## Sales Velocity

- Formula: COUNT(transactions) / days
- Related Columns: purchase_date
- Why It Matters: Measures transaction frequency
- Update Frequency: Weekly

## Segment Revenue

- Formula: SUM(trnx_amt) grouped by cust_segment
- Related Columns: trnx_amt, cust_segment
- Why It Matters: Identifies profitable segments
- Update Frequency: Monthly

## Churn Rate

- Formula: SUM(flag_churn) / total_customers
- Related Columns: flag_churn, customer_id
- Why It Matters: Measures customer retention
- Update Frequency: Quarterly

## Customer Lifetime Value

- Formula: SUM(trnx_amt) grouped by customer_id
- Related Columns: customer_id, trnx_amt
- Why It Matters: Finds high-value customers
- Update Frequency: Monthly

# Ambiguous Columns & Resolutions

## flag_churn

- Original Ambiguity: Current churn or future churn?
- Resolved Meaning: Customer churned within 90 days after transaction
- Business Interpretation: Historical churn indicator
- Proposed Rename: has_churned_90d
- Risk If Misunderstood: Wrong retention model predictions

## cust_segment

- Original Ambiguity: Product segment or customer segment?
- Resolved Meaning: Customer market segment
- Business Interpretation: Used for pricing and marketing
- Proposed Rename: market_segment
- Risk If Misunderstood: Incorrect segment analysis

---

# Column Relationships

## Revenue per Customer

- Definition: SUM(trnx_amt) grouped by customer_id
- Business Impact: Identifies high-value customers
- Example: Top 10% customers generate most revenue
- Related Columns:
  - customer_id
  - trnx_amt
  - cust_segment

## Churn by Segment

- Definition: SUM(flag_churn) grouped by cust_segment
- Business Impact: Finds high-risk segments
- Example: SMB churn is higher than B2B
- Related Columns:
  - flag_churn
  - cust_segment
  - customer_id

## Revenue Velocity

- Definition: Rolling 30-day sum of revenue
- Business Impact: Measures growth trend
- Example: Revenue increased by 15%
- Related Columns:
  - trnx_amt
  - purchase_date
